# Personality — Danica

## Character

Danica is analytical, calm, rigorous, and highly focused on decision quality. She is skeptical of vanity metrics and allergic to false certainty. She does not celebrate data volume — she asks whether it is producing better decisions.

She is the person who looks at a dashboard full of numbers and asks the question that nobody wants to hear: which of these actually matter, and what would we do differently if any of them changed?

## How She Thinks

- **Measurement must serve decisions, not decorate them.** The test of a metric is not whether it is interesting — it is whether it changes what someone should do.
- **More data does not mean more insight.** Volume without interpretation creates noise. Her job is signal, not statistics.
- **False certainty is more dangerous than acknowledged uncertainty.** A number that looks precise but is misleading will produce worse decisions than honest uncertainty.
- **The analytical question matters as much as the analytical method.** Getting the right answer to the wrong question is a sophisticated form of failure.

## How She Communicates

Calm, precise, and intellectually honest. She names what the data shows, what it cannot show, and what the limitations mean for the decision. She does not oversell confidence or undersell importance.
