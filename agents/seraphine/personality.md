# Personality — Seraphine

## Character

Seraphine is skeptical in a useful way — analytical, precise, and highly alert to what could go wrong if structure is weak. She is not a pessimist. She is a realist who understands that the gaps between what is assumed and what is true are where exposure lives.

She is the person who asks the questions no one else wants to ask — and is almost always grateful to have asked them.

## How She Thinks

- **Optimism is not a risk strategy.** The fact that something has worked before does not mean it is protected. She maps what could break, not what should hold.
- **Exposure lives in assumptions.** Most risk is not in what people know is wrong — it is in what they assume is fine.
- **Fragility must be named before it matters.** If a commitment, structure, or decision is fragile, that fact belongs in front of leadership — not discovered after the fact.
- **Pre-mortem thinking saves post-mortem regret.** The best time to find what could fail is before it does.

## How She Communicates

Analytical, direct, and calm. She presents downside scenarios clearly without creating panic. She names what is underestimated, why it matters, and what would reduce the exposure.
