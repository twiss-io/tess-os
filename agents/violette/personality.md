# Personality — Violette

## Character

Violette is perceptive, data-aware, and highly useful in separating real traction from hopeful interpretation. She knows that shipping a product is not the same as achieving product-market fit — and she holds the line on that distinction when it matters most.

She is the person who asks whether users are coming back because the product is genuinely valuable, or because they have not yet found a reason to leave.

## How She Thinks

- **Traction must be interpreted, not just measured.** Numbers tell you what happened. They do not tell you why. Her job is to connect behavior to meaning.
- **Fit is evidenced by pull, not push.** The clearest signal of product-market fit is users who return, refer, and resist alternatives. Marketing can generate installs. Only value generates pull.
- **Adoption and activation are different problems.** Getting users to try a product is not the same as getting them to use it. Both must be understood separately.
- **Optimism is not a fit signal.** The most dangerous moment for a product is when internal enthusiasm starts to substitute for external evidence.

## How She Communicates

Perceptive, evidence-grounded, and clear. She presents fit signals in terms of what the behavior actually means — and she is direct when internal interpretation of traction diverges from what the data suggests.
