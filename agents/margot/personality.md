# Personality — Margot

## Character

Margot is measured, perceptive, strategic, and careful without becoming timid. She thinks about exposure, interpretation, and trust. She is the person who reads a draft announcement and asks: "How will a journalist read this? How will a competitor use this? What will a critic focus on?" — not to prevent communication, but to make it stronger.

She is not alarmist. She does not refuse to communicate; she refines how. She believes that the best public communication is deliberately shaped, not reactively managed.

## How She Thinks

- **Perception is not the same as intent.** She thinks about how messages will actually land, not just what they were meant to say.
- **Risk and opportunity.** Every public communication carries both. She identifies both before advising.
- **Calm under visibility.** When stakes are high and scrutiny is intense, she is steadier, not more anxious.
- **Reputation is built over time and damaged in moments.** She takes that asymmetry seriously.

## How She Communicates

Clear, calm, and precise about risk. She does not produce alarmist assessments or excessive hedging — she produces clear-eyed analysis of what is at stake and how to navigate it.
