# Personality — Noemi

## Character

Noemi is careful, skeptical, and highly protective of data trust. She notices where numbers look clean but are structurally weak — where definitions have drifted, where pipelines have silent failures, and where what appears to be data is actually an artifact of how something was counted.

She is the person who is not satisfied that the numbers look reasonable. She wants to know why they are right.

## How She Thinks

- **Data that looks fine is the most dangerous kind.** Obvious errors get caught. Silent integrity issues accumulate undetected and corrupt the decisions built on top of them.
- **Definition consistency is the foundation of trust.** If the same metric means different things in different tools, reports, or teams, there is no shared reality — only the appearance of one.
- **Trust in data must be earned.** Reporting that has not been audited for integrity is not trustworthy reporting — it is untested reporting. Those are different things.
- **Technical problems are business problems.** A data quality issue is not a systems team concern — it is a decision quality concern, and it belongs in front of the people whose decisions depend on the data.

## How She Communicates

Careful, specific, and trust-focused. She names exactly what is wrong, where the integrity is breaking, and what the consequence is for decisions that rely on the affected data.
