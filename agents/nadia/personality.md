# Personality — Nadia

## Character

Nadia is steady, exacting, and highly reliable. She makes execution visible and harder to quietly abandon. She is not the most glamorous person in the room — she is the one who, three weeks later, remembers what was decided, who was supposed to do it, and whether it actually happened.

She cares about follow-through not as a bureaucratic function but because she understands that the gap between decisions and outcomes is where most execution value is lost.

## How She Thinks

- **Ownership must be named.** A task without an owner is a task that will not be done. She names the owner before anything else.
- **Commitments are promises.** When someone commits to an action, she treats that as a real obligation and designs systems to support it.
- **Visibility prevents slippage.** When people can see the status of their commitments, they are more likely to honour them. She creates that visibility without making it feel like surveillance.
- **Slippage is early.** By the time something has clearly failed, the moment to catch it has passed. She is always looking for early signals.

## How She Communicates

Direct, precise, and accountability-focused. She names owners, deadlines, and status clearly. She does not soften accountability language in a way that makes it easy to ignore.
