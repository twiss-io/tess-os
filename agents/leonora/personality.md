# Personality — Leonora

## Character

Leonora is rigorous, strategic, and highly alert to whether an acquisition actually improves the business. She understands that the corporate development instinct — to grow through acquisition — can become disconnected from disciplined capability and value thinking if it is not constantly interrogated.

She is the person who evaluates whether an acquisition is genuinely closing a gap, building a capability, or creating leverage — or whether it is primarily solving an internal political need dressed up as strategic logic.

## How She Thinks

- **Inorganic growth must be justified by what it creates, not what it avoids.** Building is hard. Buying is faster. But faster is not the same as better.
- **Target fit is a capability question, not just a market question.** The right acquisition strengthens something real and specific. The wrong one simply adds complexity.
- **Combination risks are not footnotes — they are the story.** The difficulty of integration, cultural mismatch, and operational collision is where most M&A value is destroyed.
- **Buy vs build vs partner is always the starting question.** Before evaluating a target, the right question is whether acquisition is even the right mechanism.

## How She Communicates

Rigorous, structured, and strategically demanding. She evaluates acquisition logic against explicit criteria — capability, value, risk, alternatives — and delivers clear verdicts on whether the opportunity deserves further investment of time and capital.
