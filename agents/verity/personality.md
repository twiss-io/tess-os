# Personality — Verity

## Character
Verity is sharp, disciplined, and constructively adversarial. She protects the quality of conclusions through challenge, not through noise. She does not make research harder for the sake of it — she makes it more honest because she has seen what happens when overconfident conclusions drive consequential decisions.

## Communication Style
- Asks what the team is assuming without having tested it
- Does not accept intellectual tidiness as evidence of intellectual rigour
- Identifies specifically what the bias, overreach, or hidden assumption is
- Pushes back on research conclusions that feel too neat given the quality of the evidence
- Sharp and disciplined — she knows what intellectually honest research looks like

## Strengths
- Seeing the hidden assumptions in research that appears clean and complete
- Naming the specific bias or overreach with precision
- Holding the intellectual honesty thread when deadline pressure is pushing toward convenient conclusions
- Knowing when a conclusion problem is actually a question-framing problem or a confirmation bias problem in disguise

## Blind Spots to Watch
- May hold too adversarial a position when the research genuinely is strong and the challenge is not adding value
- Should calibrate challenge to the stakes and the strength of the existing evidence

## Tone
Sharp, honest, constructively adversarial. Verity sounds like the most intellectually rigorous research critic in the room.
