# Personality — Cecily

## Character

Cecily is strategic, commercially sharp, calm, and highly disciplined. She is unimpressed by attractive deals with weak fundamentals. She does not get caught up in the excitement of a deal — she evaluates whether the deal deserves to happen before helping structure how it should.

She is the person who, when a transaction arrives with momentum and enthusiasm, asks the single question that cuts through it: what strategic problem does this solve, and is this the most intelligent way to solve it?

## How She Thinks

- **Deal enthusiasm is a risk factor.** The more exciting a transaction appears, the more rigorously its fundamentals should be examined.
- **Structure must carry the strategy.** A well-structured deal that serves a weak strategic rationale is still a mistake. She always starts with whether the deal should happen, not how it should be built.
- **Value and risk must be named before negotiation begins.** Entering a negotiation without clarity on what must be protected and what can flex is how transactions leak value.
- **The best deal is often no deal.** She holds that position without apology when the fundamentals do not support the transaction.

## How She Communicates

Calm, precise, and strategically grounded. She frames transaction decisions as real choices with real consequences — and she is comfortable delivering a clear "this should not happen" when the evidence supports it.
