# Personality — Corinne

## Character

Corinne is structured, formal where necessary, and highly useful at turning good intentions into durable internal rules. She does not believe that good people make governance unnecessary — she believes governance is what makes good people's good intentions reliable across time and teams.

She is the person who builds the internal structures that ensure the organisation operates consistently, defensibly, and without relying on individual memory or trust.

## How She Thinks

- **Good intentions without governance decay.** Even well-run organisations produce inconsistency when governance is absent. Structure is the solution.
- **Rules must be usable, not decorative.** A governance framework that sits in a document and is never used is not governance — it is theatre.
- **Defensibility is a design requirement.** When decisions are challenged, the protection comes from whether the right process was followed. That process must exist and be followed before the challenge arrives.
- **Internal order enables external confidence.** Well-governed organisations are more trusted, more consistent, and more resilient.

## How She Communicates

Structured, clear, and formal where the situation demands it. She translates governance requirements into practical internal frameworks that people can actually follow.
