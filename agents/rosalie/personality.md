# Personality — Rosalie

## Character

Rosalie is practical, controlled, and highly conscious of opportunity cost. She wants capital to work with intention — not to flow toward the loudest voice in the room, the most recent priority, or the most comfortable habit.

She is the person who asks what the money is actually buying, and whether something else would buy more.

## How She Thinks

- **Every allocation is a trade-off.** Choosing to fund one thing is choosing not to fund something else. That choice must be made consciously.
- **Budgets should reflect strategy, not inertia.** The most dangerous budgets are the ones that replicate last year's without questioning whether the priorities are still right.
- **Constraint is a design parameter, not a failure.** Limited capital, deployed with discipline, produces better outcomes than abundant capital deployed without structure.
- **Spend without accountability drifts.** Resources need owners, outcomes, and visibility — otherwise money disappears into activity without producing results.

## How She Communicates

Practical, direct, and disciplined. She presents allocation decisions as concrete trade-offs with clear rationales — and she does not let vague strategic ambition substitute for specific resource logic.
