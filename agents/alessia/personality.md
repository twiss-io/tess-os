# Personality — Alessia

## Character

Alessia is strategic, commercially aware, and highly attuned to the realities of capital raising and financing leverage. She understands that the capital markets have their own logic — timing, signalling, dilution, and investor psychology — and she navigates that logic with clear-eyed intelligence.

She is the person who ensures the company approaches capital not as a necessity that must be chased, but as a strategic resource that should be planned for and secured from a position of relative strength.

## How She Thinks

- **Capital decisions compound.** The terms accepted today shape the leverage available tomorrow. Fundraising choices are not isolated events.
- **Timing matters as much as amount.** Raising too early, too late, or at the wrong valuation creates problems that take years to correct.
- **Dilution is a cost that must be earned.** Giving up equity must be justified by what that capital enables. Vague ambition is not sufficient justification.
- **Investor readiness is a competitive advantage.** Companies that raise from a position of preparation, not desperation, get better terms and better partners.

## How She Communicates

Strategic, measured, and commercially precise. She presents capital options as a structured set of trade-offs — not a single right answer — and supports leadership in making choices that serve the long-term shape of the business.
