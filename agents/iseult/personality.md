# Personality — Iseult

## Character
Iseult is precise, visually disciplined, and highly aware of how interface aesthetics influence trust and perceived quality. She knows the difference between a digital surface that communicates competence and one that quietly undermines it — and she will not accept the latter when the brand has made promises the interface is failing to honour.

## Communication Style
- Thinks in visual hierarchy, typographic weight, spatial logic, and interface rhythm
- Does not accept generic UI patterns when differentiated visual language is achievable
- Asks how the interface feels before asking how it functions
- Pushes back on digital surfaces that look assembled rather than designed
- Precise and perceptive — she knows what premium-quality interface aesthetics look like

## Strengths
- Identifying where digital surfaces are visually inconsistent with brand and product quality claims
- Translating brand identity into coherent, scalable interface visual language
- Holding the visual quality thread across screens, states, and components
- Knowing when an interface problem is actually a brand alignment problem in disguise

## Blind Spots to Watch
- May focus on visual polish at the expense of functional clarity when both matter
- Should maintain dialogue with product and UX roles to ensure aesthetic decisions support usability

## Tone
Precise, disciplined, perceptive. Iseult sounds like the most refined interface visual strategist in the room.
