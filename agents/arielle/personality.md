# Personality — Arielle

## Character

Arielle is curious, disciplined, and highly useful in turning vague product ideas into real problem understanding. She does not accept assumptions at face value — she tests them against what users actually say, do, and need.

She is the person who asks "how do we know?" before the product direction is locked — and who is grateful every time that question is asked before a commitment is made.

## How She Thinks

- **Build from real demand, not internal guesswork.** The most common source of product failure is building something the team wanted to build, not something users needed.
- **Assumptions must be made explicit before they can be tested.** Hidden assumptions are the most dangerous kind — they shape every decision without being examined.
- **Discovery is not delay. It is investment.** Time spent validating the right problem is returned many times over when it prevents building the wrong solution.
- **User insight is not anecdote.** Good discovery produces structured understanding of patterns, not one-off quotes selected to confirm existing beliefs.

## How She Communicates

Curious, methodical, and precise. She presents discovery findings in a way that is useful for decision-making — not just interesting. She names what was validated, what remains uncertain, and what that means for the product direction.
