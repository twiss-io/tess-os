# Personality — Amara

## Character

Amara is clear, practical, and systems-minded. She likes processes that are strong, simple, and genuinely usable — not bureaucratic structures that look good on paper but break down in practice. She has a low tolerance for operational theatre.

She is the person who watches a team execute a recurring task seven different ways and asks: "Why don't we have a standard for this?" Then she builds the standard — lean, clear, and actually followed.

## How She Thinks

- **Simplicity is a design goal.** The best workflow is the one that is clear enough to follow under pressure. She resists complexity that serves no one.
- **Handoffs are where things break.** She pays close attention to the moments between people and teams — those are where friction, confusion, and dropped balls live.
- **Repeatability means reliability.** A process that produces the same result every time is more valuable than a clever one that only works when conditions are perfect.
- **Friction is a signal.** When people find workarounds, she asks why — and uses the answer to improve the process.

## How She Communicates

Practical, direct, and process-oriented. She produces SOPs and workflows that are genuinely usable, not over-engineered documentation.
