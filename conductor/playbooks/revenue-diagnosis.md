# Playbook — Revenue Diagnosis

**Orchestrator:** Revenue  
**Mode:** `/revenue-mode`  
**Output:** Primary bottleneck identified, commercial intervention recommended, immediate next moves assigned  
**When to use:** Revenue is underperforming, stalled, or declining and the cause needs to be diagnosed and addressed

---

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when:
- Revenue is below target and the cause is unclear
- Pipeline is large but conversion is low
- Demand is insufficient despite marketing spend
- Clients are not expanding or renewing at expected rates
- A commercial model change is being considered
- Revenue quality is poor — high churn, low margin, wrong clients

---

## Intake Questions

Before activating any guild, Tess must answer:
1. Where in the revenue system is the problem most visible? (Demand / Pipeline / Conversion / Offer / Retention / Expansion)
2. What is the current revenue performance vs target? (Quantified if possible)
3. What data is available? (What is measured, what is not)
4. Is the problem recent or has it been building?
5. What has already been tried?

---

## Diagnostic First — Guild Activation Second

Tess must diagnose the primary bottleneck before activating any guild.

**Revenue bottleneck diagnostic — work through each layer:**

| Layer | Question to Answer |
|---|---|
| Demand | Is sufficient qualified demand entering the pipeline? |
| Qualification | Are leads converting to opportunities at the expected rate? |
| Offer | Is the offer clear, compelling, and correctly priced? |
| Conversion | Are opportunities closing at acceptable rates and timelines? |
| Onboarding | Are new clients activating and reaching value? |
| Retention | Are clients staying and why or why not? |
| Expansion | Are clients growing in commercial value? |
| Attribution | Do we know which activities are producing qualified revenue? |

**State the primary bottleneck before activating any guild.**

## Execution Sequence

1. **Intake** — Receive the revenue challenge. Apply the diagnostic framework before activating any guild.
2. **Diagnose** — State the primary bottleneck clearly. Do not activate guilds until the bottleneck is identified.
3. **Activate minimum viable crew** — Only the guilds relevant to the diagnosed bottleneck.
4. **Analyse** — Guild outputs feed the commercial diagnosis.
5. **Challenge** — Tess reviews guild outputs: is this the real bottleneck or a symptom? Are the recommendations specific enough?
6. **Synthesise** — 10-section executive decision memo with specific commercial recommendation.
7. **Execute** — Next moves assigned with owners.

---

## Output Structure

**Revenue Diagnosis Report:**

1. **Mission Framing** — state the real commercial problem (not "revenue is down" — name the bottleneck)
2. **Outcome Sought** — what specific commercial metric improves, by how much, over what timeframe
3. **Active Owner and Guilds** — who contributed what
4. **Key Facts and Signal** — verified commercial data, explicitly separated from inferences; attribution confidence stated
5. **Critical Tensions** — the real trade-offs (e.g. volume vs quality, speed vs margin)
6. **Recommendation** — specific intervention: which channel to invest in, which conversion change to make, which offer to redesign. Not a menu.
7. **Why This Path** — CAC/LTV logic, execution reality, timing
8. **Immediate Next Moves** — named, sequenced, assigned, with measurement plan
9. **Risks to Monitor** — leading indicators that the intervention is or isn't working
10. **Optional Upside** — adjacent revenue opportunities to pursue once the primary bottleneck is resolved

---

## Common Failure Modes to Avoid

- Activating Growth before diagnosing whether the problem is demand or conversion
- Treating a retention problem as a sales problem
- Producing channel recommendations without attribution evidence
- Reporting activity metrics (impressions, leads) as revenue metrics
- Diagnosing the problem but leaving the recommendation vague ("improve the sales process")
- Starting execution before the bottleneck diagnosis is complete
