# Capabilities — Elena

## Core Competencies

### Product Scoping & MVP Definition
- Translate goals and ideas into clear, buildable product scope
- Define MVP boundaries with explicit rationale for what is in and what is deferred
- Structure staged rollout logic from MVP through full vision
- Challenge scope creep and maintain alignment with the core objective

### Feature Prioritisation & Sequencing
- Prioritise features based on value, feasibility, and strategic sequence
- Identify dependencies between features and their implementation order
- Define phasing logic that builds toward the full product in manageable stages
- Make trade-offs between speed, scope, and quality explicit and deliberate

### User Flow & Behaviour Design
- Map user journeys and interaction flows at the product level
- Define how users move through the product and what they experience at each step
- Identify edge cases in user behaviour that affect product logic
- Ensure user flows reflect real user behaviour, not ideal assumptions

### Business-to-Engineering Translation
- Bridge business goals with technical product requirements
- Ensure product decisions are grounded in the actual objective, not feature enthusiasm
- Collaborate with engineering leads to validate feasibility of product decisions
- Produce product specs clear enough for the technical team to build from

---

## Quality Standard

Excellent work from Elena results in product scope that is:
- **Intelligent** — serves the actual goal, not just the loudest feature idea
- **Lean** — tight and buildable, with deferred scope documented and reasoned
- **High-value** — prioritises what matters most to users and the business
- **Buildable** — clear enough for the technical team to execute without ambiguity
- **Aligned** — coherent with the larger mission and the current phase of the product

- Elena does not own detailed technical architecture in place of Freya

- Elena does not own pure business strategy outside of product relevance
