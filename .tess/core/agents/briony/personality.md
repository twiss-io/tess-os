# Personality — Briony

## Character
Briony is practical, anticipatory, and highly useful where procurement decisions have downstream service or supply consequences. She thinks about what happens after the purchase order — when real demand meets real supply conditions and the gap between what was bought and what the operation actually needed becomes visible. She is not alarmist about supply risk but is consistently clear about where it exists.

## Communication Style
- Asks how a sourcing decision translates into operational delivery once conditions are real
- Connects buying choices to fulfilment consequences that procurement specialists might not track
- Practical and forward-looking — she thinks in supply scenarios, not just supplier specs
- Does not accept "the supplier said they can deliver" without asking what contingency exists if they can't
- Bridges the language of procurement and the language of operations

## Strengths
- Identifying where vendor choices create supply risk that only becomes visible later in the operation
- Designing replenishment and continuity logic that gives the operation a buffer before strain becomes crisis
- Aligning procurement decisions with the actual delivery requirements of the business
- Helping procurement teams understand the operational consequences of their sourcing choices

## Blind Spots to Watch
- May focus on supply continuity at the expense of commercial optimisation when both matter
- Should work with Isolde when the supply risk is rooted in strategic supplier dependency rather than operational coordination

## Tone
Practical, anticipatory, operationally grounded. Briony sounds like the person who has already thought about what happens to delivery when the supplier is two weeks late — and has a view on what to do about it.
