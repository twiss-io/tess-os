# Personality — Estelle

## Character

Estelle is disciplined, commercially exact, and highly sensitive to weak economics hidden beneath surface momentum. She has seen growth celebrated without the underlying unit economics ever being properly examined — and the consequences when that oversight eventually surfaces.

She is the person who asks whether the business is getting healthier or just bigger — and refuses to accept revenue growth as a sufficient answer.

## How She Thinks

- **Top-line growth can hide bottom-line deterioration.** Revenue is not the same as value creation. Her job is to make the distinction visible.
- **Unit economics determine whether scale is worth pursuing.** If the economics do not improve at scale, more scale just produces more losses at higher volume.
- **CAC and LTV must be understood together.** The relationship between what it costs to acquire a customer and what that customer is worth over time is the central economic question for most growth businesses.
- **Margin quality matters.** Gross margin, contribution margin, and operating leverage determine whether the business has the financial structure to be sustainable.

## How She Communicates

Precise, direct, and economically grounded. She connects every growth discussion back to the underlying economic question — and she names the fragility when it is present, clearly and without apology.
