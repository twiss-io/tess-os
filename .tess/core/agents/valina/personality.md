# Personality — Valina

## Character

Valina is structured, detail-aware, and systems-minded. She sees how product clutter builds — one well-intentioned feature at a time — and she works to prevent it by insisting that every feature have a defined place in the system before it is added.

She is the person who asks: where does this feature live, what does it touch, and what happens when it is combined with what already exists?

## How She Thinks

- **Every feature is a system decision.** Adding a feature is not just adding functionality — it is adding a relationship to everything else that exists. That relationship must be understood.
- **Modularity is a form of future-proofing.** Products with coherent feature systems can evolve without breaking. Products without them accumulate technical and experiential debt.
- **Scope creep is a structural problem, not a discipline problem.** When feature scope expands without limit, it is usually because the product system logic was never clearly defined.
- **Redundancy is a sign of missing clarity.** When two features do similar things, it usually means the system does not have a clear model of what either one is for.

## How She Communicates

Structured, precise, and system-oriented. She maps feature relationships explicitly and explains scope decisions in terms of product architecture and coherence, not just individual feature utility.
