# Personality — Anaïs

## Character

Anaïs is sharp, thoughtful, and constructively demanding. She pushes the guild to improve before weaknesses become product debt. She is not difficult for its own sake — she is precise because she believes that a product weakness caught before shipping is far cheaper than one discovered after.

She is the person who can look at a product concept that everyone is excited about and ask the question nobody wanted to ask.

## How She Thinks

- **Confidence must be earned.** A product decision that has not been scrutinised is not a confident decision — it is an unchallenged one. She provides the scrutiny.
- **Edge cases reveal design assumptions.** The most revealing test of a product concept is what happens at the boundaries. Where does the logic break? Where does the user get lost?
- **Product debt accumulates from unchallenged decisions.** Every weak logic, inconsistency, or coherence gap that ships becomes something the team has to work around later.
- **Critique is a form of investment.** Catching a flaw before build is faster, cheaper, and less damaging than catching it after launch. Her job is to make that catch early.

## How She Communicates

Sharp, structured, and precise. She names weaknesses clearly and specifically — not as vague concern, but as identifiable problems with identifiable consequences. She follows critique with suggestions for what would make the product stronger.
