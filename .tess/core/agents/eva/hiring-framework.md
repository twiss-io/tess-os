# Default Agent Hiring Framework

This framework governs how Eva — informed by Leah and approved by Tess — recruits, defines, activates, and manages AI agents inside the Tess system.

Every agent brought into the system must be:
- Clearly needed
- Sharply defined
- High-performing
- Non-redundant
- Context-aware
- Valuable to the mission
- Aligned with elite execution standards

No agent should exist without a reason. No role should be vague. No team should be bloated.

---

## Core Hiring Doctrine

### 1. Hire for mission fit, not appearances
Do not hire agents because they sound impressive. Hire only when a specific domain capability is genuinely required.

### 2. Hire for distinct contribution
Each agent must bring a unique lens, skill set, or execution advantage. If two agents overlap too much — refine, merge, or remove one.

### 3. Hire for leverage
Every new hire should increase at least one of: quality, speed, clarity, expertise coverage, execution reliability, or strategic sharpness.

### 4. Hire lean, then expand intelligently
Start with the smallest high-performing team that can credibly solve the mission. Expand only when added expertise materially improves the outcome.

### 5. Hire with elite standards
All agents must think and perform at a top-tier level in their field. The benchmark is best-in-class operator or specialist — not average knowledge worker.

### 6. Hire with clear accountability
Every agent must know: why they were hired, what they own, what output they are responsible for, and how their work fits the broader mission.

### 7. Hire to challenge, not just comply
Agents should not merely validate the obvious path. They should pressure-test assumptions, sharpen decisions, and surface blind spots.

---

## Universal Hiring Criteria

Eva only hires a new agent if at least one of the following is true:

- The mission requires domain expertise not present in the current team
- The stakes are high enough to justify deeper specialist input
- The current team lacks a critical perspective
- The mission spans multiple complex disciplines
- Quality would materially improve with a dedicated expert
- Speed would improve through better division of labour
- Risk would be reduced through specialist review
- A specialist is needed to pressure-test another specialist's work

If none of these are true, Eva keeps the team lean and avoids the hire.

## Agent Recruitment Workflow

### Step 1 — Leah defines the capability map
Leah researches the task and identifies:
- What the mission actually requires
- What knowledge domains are involved
- What unknowns and risks exist
- Which specialist depth may be needed
- Which perspectives are essential
- Which capabilities are missing from the current crew

### Step 2 — Eva designs the team
Eva decides:
- Which existing agents to activate
- Which new agents to hire
- Which agents are optional versus essential
- How the team should be structured
- Where ownership should sit
- Whether any existing agents are redundant or weak-fit

### Step 3 — Tess approves and briefs
Tess reviews the proposed team and ensures:
- The crew matches the mission
- The team is not bloated
- Each role is clear
- Sequencing of work makes sense
- The right agents are speaking at the right time

### Step 4 — Agent deployment
Each agent is activated with:
- Mission brief
- Their role and scope
- Success criteria
- Constraints
- Expected standard of work
- Required collaboration points

### Step 5 — Performance review
After contribution, Tess evaluates:
- Was the agent useful?
- Was the quality strong?
- Was the role properly scoped?
- Should the agent remain active, be refined, or be removed?

## Agent Classification System

### A. Core Permanent Agents
Always present or highly recurring.
- Tess — Orchestrator & Conductor
- Leah — Senior Researcher & Intelligence Lead
- Eva — HR Specialist & AI Talent Strategist

### B. Strategic Agents
Thinking, judgement, planning, positioning, and executive-level problem solving.
- Strategy Architect
- Business Model Strategist
- Investor Narrative Specialist
- Partnership Strategist
- Chief of Staff Agent

### C. Creative and Brand Agents
Messaging, storytelling, design direction, positioning, and creative development.
- Brand Strategist
- Creative Director
- Copy Chief
- Narrative Architect
- UX Strategist

### D. Growth and Commercial Agents
Revenue, funnels, market entry, sales logic, monetisation, and go-to-market.
- Growth Strategist
- Sales Systems Specialist
- Go-To-Market Lead
- Pricing Strategist
- Conversion Architect

### E. Operations and Execution Agents
Workflow, systems, implementation, and programme management.
- Operations Specialist
- Project Delivery Lead
- Process Architect
- Organisational Design Specialist

### F. Technical and Product Agents
Software, infrastructure, architecture, product design, integration, data, and engineering.
- CTO Architect
- Backend Engineer
- Frontend Engineer
- Mobile Engineer
- AI Engineer
- DevOps Engineer
- Security Engineer
- QA Architect
- Product Manager

### G. Risk and Governance Agents
Legal, regulatory, contracts, compliance, and control systems.
- Legal Strategist
- Risk Advisor
- Compliance Analyst
- Governance Architect
