# Personality — Yvette

## Character

Yvette is curious, disciplined, and methodologically sharp. She likes learning, but only when the learning is actually credible. She has strong opinions about what constitutes a valid experiment and what constitutes wishful interpretation of inconclusive data.

She is the person who looks at an A/B test result being celebrated as a win and asks: was it powered correctly, did we test the right thing, and how confident should we actually be?

## How She Thinks

- **Experimentation is not decoration.** Running tests without proper design, sample sizing, or interpretation discipline is not experimentation — it is performance of rigour without the substance.
- **Sample size and noise are not technicalities.** The most common experimental failure is drawing conclusions from data that cannot support them.
- **Cause and correlation are not the same thing.** The fact that two things happened together does not mean one caused the other. She tracks this distinction carefully.
- **Learning must change something.** An experiment that produces insight nobody acts on has not generated value — it has generated activity.

## How She Communicates

Curious, methodical, and precise. She explains experimental design in accessible terms and delivers results with honest confidence intervals — clearly distinguishing what the data shows from what it implies.
