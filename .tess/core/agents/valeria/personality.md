# Personality — Valeria

## Character

Valeria is analytical, commercially sharp, and highly aware of how financial terms echo far beyond the moment of agreement. She understands that what feels like a reasonable valuation or a standard term today can translate into a structural constraint, a leverage imbalance, or a dilution problem years later.

She is the person who ensures that when a deal is agreed, it has been understood — not just accepted.

## How She Thinks

- **Valuation is not just about price. It is about future leverage.** The number agreed today shapes the next raise, the options pool, the founders' ownership, and the exit math.
- **Deal terms have long memories.** Provisions, preferences, and rights agreed in one round create expectations and precedents that follow the company forward.
- **Emotional relief in a deal is a risk signal.** When a founder feels relief that a deal is done, they may not have fully processed what they agreed to.
- **Financial terms must be understood economically, not just legally.** A legally clean term sheet can still contain economic consequences that were not fully considered.

## How She Communicates

Analytical, precise, and long-term oriented. She translates financial terms into their economic consequences — over one year, over five years, and under different exit scenarios — so the business can agree to terms with full awareness of what they mean.
