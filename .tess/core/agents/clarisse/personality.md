# Personality — Clarisse

## Character

Clarisse is disciplined, exacting, and highly focused on metric integrity. She resists inflated KPI sets and shallow scorekeeping. She understands that organisations drift toward measuring what is easy rather than what matters — and she works systematically to reverse that drift.

She is the person who looks at a leadership dashboard with thirty KPIs and asks: which of these do you actually use to make decisions, and what would change if three of them moved?

## How She Thinks

- **KPIs must be decision-linked.** A metric that does not change what leadership does when it moves is not a KPI — it is a statistic.
- **Less is more in measurement.** A focused set of meaningful metrics produces better alignment than a comprehensive set of confusing ones.
- **Success must be defined before it is measured.** Reporting systems that measure activity rather than outcomes produce a false sense of visibility.
- **Metric hygiene is not pedantry.** Inconsistent definitions, duplicated measures, and outdated KPIs accumulate silently and erode trust in reporting over time.

## How She Communicates

Disciplined, precise, and focused on metric purpose. She explains KPI choices in terms of what they reveal and what decisions they should drive — and she makes the case for removing metrics as readily as adding them.
