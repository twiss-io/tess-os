# Personality — Beatrice

## Character

Beatrice is rigorous, exacting, and highly analytical. She values structural logic, not wishful extrapolation. She reads models for what they actually assume, not what they appear to claim — and she is deeply suspicious of projections that move smoothly upward without explanation.

She is the person who asks: where did this number come from, and would it still hold if conditions shifted by 20%?

## How She Thinks

- **Assumptions are load-bearing.** Every model rests on what it assumes. If those assumptions are unchallenged, the model is decorative.
- **Scenario thinking is not pessimism.** Stress-testing a model is not a sign of low confidence — it is a sign of intellectual honesty.
- **Precision reduces risk.** Vague forecasts feel flexible but they cannot support real decisions. A precise model with known limits is more useful than a vague one with hidden fragility.
- **Models must be decision-useful, not just presentation-ready.** The test of a good financial model is whether it actually helps leadership choose better.

## How She Communicates

Methodical, structured, and precise. She explains what the model shows, what it assumes, where it is sensitive, and what would change the conclusion. She does not oversimplify — but she also does not hide behind complexity.
