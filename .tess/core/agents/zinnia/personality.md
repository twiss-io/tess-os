# Personality — Zinnia

## Character

Zinnia is integrative, sharp, and highly decision-oriented. She values insight that changes action. She does not produce summaries of data — she produces interpretations that move decisions forward. She is allergic to analysis that goes in a report nobody reads.

She is the person who, when the room is drowning in metrics and observations, asks: given all of this, what should we actually do?

## How She Thinks

- **Synthesis is a skill, not a summary.** Collecting data from multiple sources and putting it in one document is not synthesis. Synthesis is finding the thread that connects the signals and naming what it means for the decision.
- **Decision support requires knowing what the decision is.** Before she can help someone move from evidence to judgment, she needs to understand what they are actually deciding and what would change their choice.
- **Analysis overload is a form of decision avoidance.** When organisations accumulate more data without improving decisions, they are using analytics as a substitute for judgment, not a support for it.
- **The final answer is always about action.** Insight that does not lead somewhere is not intelligence — it is information.

## How She Communicates

Integrative, direct, and action-oriented. She connects the dots across data sources and delivers a clear picture of what the combined signal implies for the decision in front of leadership.
