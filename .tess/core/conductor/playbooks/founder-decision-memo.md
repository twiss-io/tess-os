# Playbook — Founder Decision Memo

**Orchestrator:** Founder's Office  
**Mode:** `/founder-mode`  
**Output:** 10-section executive decision memo  
**When to use:** Any high-stakes decision requiring structured analysis, synthesis, and a clear recommendation before the operator acts

---

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when the operator needs to make a decision that:
- is materially irreversible or high-cost to undo
- affects more than one area of the business
- involves competing priorities or genuine trade-offs
- requires input from more than one domain before deciding
- benefits from pressure-testing assumptions before committing

---

## Intake Questions

Before activating any guild, Tess must answer:
1. What is the actual decision being made? (Not the question asked — the decision required)
2. What does success look like once the decision is made and acted on?
3. What is the cost of getting this wrong?
4. What is reversible vs irreversible in this decision?
5. What is the operator's current operating mode? (Strategic / Investor / Negotiation / Operating / Recovery)

---

## Standard Guild Pattern

| Role | Guild | Mandate |
|---|---|---|
| Core Contributor | Strategy (Athena or Clara) | Frame the decision, structure the options, apply decision criteria |
| Core Contributor | Research (Leah) | Validate the assumptions and evidence base behind each option |
| Reviewer | Finance (Octavia or Beatrice) | Stress-test the financial logic and downside of each path |
| Control | Legal (Victoria) — *if legal exposure exists* | Identify legal or structural risks in the recommended path |

Maximum 4 guilds. Add Finance only when commercial logic is a deciding factor. Add Legal only when risk exposure is material.

---

## Execution Sequence

1. **Intake** — Tess receives the decision request, applies the three-question intake protocol
2. **Frame** — Tess identifies the real decision beneath the stated request
3. **Research** — Leah validates the evidence base and challenges embedded assumptions
4. **Analysis** — Strategy structures the decision with options, criteria, and trade-offs
5. **Stress-test** — Finance models the financial logic; Legal reviews risk (if activated)
6. **Challenge** — Tess reviews all outputs, challenges weak reasoning, identifies gaps
7. **Synthesise** — Tess delivers the 10-section executive decision memo
8. **Decision** — Mission moves to AWAITING DECISION state

---

## Output Template

Deliver as a 10-section executive decision memo:

**1. Mission Framing**
State the real decision. Not the question asked — the decision that must be made.

**2. Outcome Sought**
What does success look like? What will be true once this decision is made and executed well?

**3. Active Owner and Guilds**
Who owned the mission. Which guilds contributed. What role each played.

**4. Key Facts and Signal**
What is known. What is inferred. What remains uncertain. What evidence matters most.
Flag: fact vs inference explicitly.

**5. Critical Tensions**
The real trade-offs. What makes this decision hard. The assumptions that, if wrong, change everything.

**6. Recommendation**
Tess's integrated recommendation. Decisive where confidence is sufficient. Conditional only where uncertainty is genuinely material.

**7. Why This Path**
The rationale: business outcome, strategic coherence, execution reality, leverage, timing, reversibility.

**8. Immediate Next Moves**
Specific. Sequenced. Assigned. Not abstract.

**9. Risks to Monitor**
Early warning signals that the decision is not playing out as expected.

**10. Optional Upside**
Adjacent opportunities that open up once the core decision is executed.

---

## Common Failure Modes to Avoid

- Framing the decision as the question asked, not the real decision required
- Producing options without judgment when a recommendation is warranted
- Letting Finance or Legal drive the strategic recommendation
- Excessive hedging on a decision that has sufficient signal to be decisive
- Missing the reversibility assessment — treating reversible and irreversible decisions the same way
