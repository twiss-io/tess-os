# Playbook — Investor and Fundraising Prep

**Orchestrator:** Founder's Office  
**Mode:** `/founder-mode`  
**Output:** Investor narrative, pitch structure, financial framing, and preparation brief  
**When to use:** Preparing for a fundraising round, investor meeting, board presentation, or any external capital conversation

---

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when:
- the operator is preparing for a fundraising round (seed, Series A/B/C, bridge)
- An investor meeting requires preparation and narrative clarity
- Board materials or investor updates need to be created
- Deal framing or valuation logic needs to be developed
- The investor story needs to be stress-tested before a high-stakes meeting

---

## Intake Questions

Before activating any guild, Tess must answer:
1. What is the audience? (New investors / existing investors / board / strategic partners)
2. What is the objective of this specific interaction? (Lead generation / due diligence / close / update)
3. What stage and structure is the raise? (Amount, instrument, valuation expectations)
4. What is the current narrative — what story does the business tell about itself?
5. What is the strongest version of the investment thesis?
6. What are the hardest questions this investor will ask?

## Execution Sequence

1. **Intake** — Classify the fundraising objective. What specifically needs to be produced?
2. **Thesis formation** — What is the investment thesis in one sentence? What makes this business a compelling bet?
3. **Narrative architecture** — Finance + Messaging structure the story: why now, why us, why this market, what the capital unlocks
4. **Financial grounding** — Finance builds or reviews the financial model: current state, projections, unit economics, use of funds
5. **Stress-test** — Leah validates market claims. Strategy checks competitive framing. Tess identifies the hardest questions and prepares responses.
6. **Voice calibration** — Messaging ensures the narrative sounds like the operator, not a generic pitch
7. **Synthesise** — Tess delivers the fundraising preparation brief
8. **Rehearsal framing** — Tess identifies the 5 questions most likely to be asked and frames response positions

---

## Output Structure

**Investor Narrative Brief:**
1. The investment thesis — one sentence, sharp
2. The problem and the market — why this matters and why now
3. The solution and the business — what we do and why it works
4. Traction and proof — what has been proven and at what scale
5. The team — why the operator and this team
6. The financials — current state, trajectory, unit economics
7. Use of funds — what this capital enables specifically
8. The ask — amount, structure, valuation logic, timeline

**Preparation Brief:**
- The 5 hardest questions and recommended response positions
- Competitive positioning for likely investor comparisons
- Key numbers to know cold
- Any vulnerabilities to pre-empt rather than react to

---

## Common Failure Modes to Avoid

- Building the narrative before the thesis is sharp
- Financial projections that are not grounded in real unit economics
- Market size claims that cannot be defended under pressure
- Founder voice that sounds like a template, not like the operator
- Preparing for soft questions instead of the hard ones
- Ignoring deal structure and terms until late in the process
