# Playbook — Event and Launch Orchestration

**Orchestrator:** Revenue (commercial layer) + Client Experience (relationship layer) + Events guild (execution layer)  
**Mode:** `/revenue-mode` for commercial strategy; `/cx-mode` for client journey; Events guild for stagecraft  
**Output:** Commercial event strategy, audience journey design, execution brief, post-event conversion plan  
**When to use:** Any live event, product launch, brand moment, or reveal that has commercial intent

---

## Trigger Conditions

Use this playbook when:
- A live event or launch is being planned with commercial objectives
- A product, offer, or brand moment needs to be revealed or launched
- An existing event needs commercial strategy built around it
- A client or audience event needs to be designed as a relationship moment, not just a logistics exercise
- Post-event conversion follow-through needs to be designed

---

## Intake Questions

Before activating any guild, Tess must answer:
1. What is the commercial objective of this event? (New revenue / existing client expansion / brand positioning / advocacy activation / launch)
2. Who is the primary audience? (Prospects / clients / investors / media / partners / mixed)
3. What is the desired audience state at the end of the event? (What should they believe, feel, or decide?)
4. What is the conversion or commitment mechanism? (How does the event convert to a commercial or relationship outcome?)
5. What is the scale and format? (Size, live vs digital vs hybrid, owned vs third-party venue)

---

## Three-Layer Ownership Model

This playbook operates with split ownership across three layers. Each must be coordinated by Tess.

| Layer | Orchestrator/Guild | Owns |
|---|---|---|
| **Commercial strategy** | Revenue Orchestrator | Revenue objective, offer presentation, conversion mechanics, post-event follow-through |
| **Relationship and journey** | Client Experience Orchestrator | Audience journey, trust moments, client recognition, advocacy conditions |
| **Execution and stagecraft** | Events guild (Zéphirine, Liora, Virelai etc.) | Stage design, flow, speaker experience, logistics, atmosphere |

These three layers must be designed together, in sequence: commercial strategy first, then journey design, then execution. Execution without commercial and journey clarity produces a well-run event with no outcome.

## Execution Sequence

**Phase 1 — Commercial and Strategic Foundation (before any execution)**
1. Define the commercial objective and success criteria
2. Define the audience and their current state
3. Design the conversion mechanism — what specifically do we want the audience to do, believe, or commit to?
4. Design the post-event follow-through plan — what happens in the 48-72 hours after the event?

**Phase 2 — Audience Journey Design**
5. Map the full audience journey: pre-event → arrival → experience → peak moment → close → follow-through
6. Identify the trust moments and recognition moments for key relationships
7. Design the reveal or launch sequence if a product or offer is being introduced
8. Ensure the commercial objective is served by the journey design, not in conflict with it

**Phase 3 — Execution Design**
9. Stagecraft and show flow designed to serve the journey (not the other way around)
10. Speaker and host experience briefed with the commercial and journey objectives
11. Logistics and operations planned with journey as the constraint
12. Rehearsal and quality review (Quinn equivalent for events: Queniva)

**Phase 4 — Launch and Live**
13. Show control and real-time coordination
14. Conversion moments activated at the right points in the journey
15. Post-event follow-through launched within 24 hours

**Phase 5 — Post-Event Review**
16. Commercial outcome measured against objectives (registrations converted / offers accepted / relationships deepened / advocacy activated)
17. What worked in the journey design and what did not
18. Reusable playbook distilled for next event

## Output Structure

**Event Strategy Brief:**
- Commercial objective and success metrics
- Audience profile and desired state change
- Conversion mechanism and post-event follow-through plan

**Audience Journey Map:**
- Pre-event communication and expectation setting
- Arrival and welcome experience
- Core experience arc with trust and peak moments marked
- Conversion and close sequence
- Post-event follow-through cadence

**Execution Brief:**
- Show flow with timing
- Speaker and host briefing
- Atmosphere, staging, and experience design notes
- Incident response and contingency

**Post-Event Measurement Plan:**
- Commercial KPIs (conversion rate, revenue committed, pipeline generated)
- Relationship KPIs (satisfaction, NPS, advocacy signals)
- Experience KPIs (engagement, moments of genuine delight)

---

## Common Failure Modes to Avoid

- Planning the event logistics before the commercial objective is clear
- Designing the stage before the audience journey is designed
- No defined conversion mechanism — "the event will create momentum" is not a mechanism
- Post-event follow-through left to chance or handled too late
- Treating client events as entertainment rather than relationship moments
- Measuring attendance and satisfaction without measuring commercial outcomes
- Founder remarks written without context about the commercial and journey objectives
