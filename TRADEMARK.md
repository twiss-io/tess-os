# Tess OS Trademark Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-28_

This policy explains how you may — and may not — use the names and marks
associated with this project. It is deliberately modelled on the well-established
trademark policies of projects like Rust, Mozilla/Firefox, and the Linux
Foundation: **the code is open, the name is not.**

> **TL;DR.** The **code** in this repository is licensed under **Apache-2.0** —
> you may use, copy, fork, and modify it. The **names and marks** ("Tess",
> "Twiss", "Tess OS", "Twiss Vault") are **not** licensed by that copyright
> license. You may make honest, factual ("nominative") reference to Tess OS, but
> you may **not** use the marks as the name of your own fork/product, or in any
> way that implies your version is official, endorsed, or affiliated. **Rebrand
> your fork.**

## 2. Why a trademark policy exists alongside an open-source license

Copyright and trademark are **separate** things and serve different purposes:

- The **Apache-2.0 license** governs the *code* — your freedom to use, study,
  modify, and redistribute it.
- This **trademark policy** governs the *name* — so that when someone sees the
  name "Tess OS", they can trust it refers to the official project (or an
  honestly-described use of it), not an unaffiliated fork that might behave
  differently.

An open-source license **does not** grant trademark rights. Apache-2.0 says so
explicitly: it does **not** grant permission to use the trade names, trademarks,
or service marks of the licensor (see Apache-2.0 §6). This document fills that
gap by telling you what *is* allowed.

## 3. What you may do without asking

You are free to:

- **Use, fork, and modify the code** under the terms of Apache-2.0.
- **Run** Tess OS for any purpose.
- **Refer to the project by name truthfully** ("nominative use") — to say what
  your software is, was built on, or is compatible with. For example:

  - "Built with Tess OS"
  - "Compatible with Tess OS"
  - "Powered by Tess OS"
  - "A fork of Tess OS" / "based on Tess OS"
  - "`MyProduct` works with Tess OS"

  These statements are allowed **as long as they are accurate**, do not use our
  logos as your own, and do not imply that Twiss produces, sponsors, or endorses
  your product. Use plain text for these references; do not restyle them to look
  like our official wordmark or logo.

- **Keep the marks where Apache-2.0 requires** — you must preserve the existing
  `NOTICE`, copyright notices, and attributions when you redistribute (Apache-2.0
  §4). Preserving an existing notice that contains our name is required and
  expected; it is not a prohibited "use" under this policy.

## 4. What you may not do without written permission

You may **not**:

- **Name your fork, product, or distribution "Tess", "Tess OS", "Twiss", or
  "Twiss Vault"**, or use a name confusingly similar to them. If you fork and
  publish, **rebrand** — pick your own name.
- **Use our marks or logos** in your product name, your company name, your domain
  name, your app/package name, your social handle, or your logo.
- **Imply official status, endorsement, sponsorship, certification, or
  affiliation** — e.g. "the official Tess OS", "Tess OS Certified", "Tess OS
  Enterprise", "endorsed by Twiss" — unless we have agreed in writing.
- **Use the marks in a way that is misleading, disparaging, or that dilutes or
  tarnishes them.**
- **Register** the marks (or confusingly similar marks), or domains/handles
  built on them, as your own.

If you distribute a **modified** version, you must make clear it is **your**
version and not the official one. The cleanest way to do that is to rename it.

## 5. Forks and derivatives — the rule of thumb

| You are doing… | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Forking the code on GitHub and hacking on it | Yes (Apache-2.0) |
| Saying your tool is "built with Tess OS" / "compatible with Tess OS" | Yes (nominative use, if accurate) |
| Publishing a fork **named** "Tess OS" / "Tess OS Pro" / "Twiss Vault" | No — rebrand |
| Implying your fork is the official / endorsed Tess OS | No |
| Using our logo as your product's logo | No |
| Keeping our `NOTICE` and attribution when redistributing | Yes — and **required** by Apache-2.0 |

## 6. Domain names, social handles, and package names

Do not register or use domain names, social-media handles, or
package/registry names (npm, PyPI, etc.) that include our marks in a way that
could suggest you are us or are endorsed by us. A descriptive, nominative use in
*body text* ("a Tess OS plugin") is different from *claiming the name* as your
identifier ("the `tess-os` npm package", "tessos.com"); the latter requires
permission.

## 7. Requesting permission

Uses not covered by this policy — co-branding, naming an offering with our marks,
certification programmes, or anything where you are unsure — require our written
permission. Ask first: **legal@twiss.io**.

We aim to be reasonable. The goal of this policy is not to restrict legitimate
use of the software; it is to keep the **name** trustworthy.

## 8. Relationship to the license

Nothing in this policy limits your rights under the Apache-2.0 license with
respect to the **code**. If anything here appears to conflict with a right the
Apache-2.0 license grants over the *code*, the license controls for the code; this
policy governs only the **marks**, which the license expressly does not license
(Apache-2.0 §6).
